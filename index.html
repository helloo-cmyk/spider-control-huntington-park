Common Spider Species in Huntington Park

Understanding the local spiders in your house helps identify potential safety risks.

Black Widow

Venomous / High Risk

Easily recognized by the shiny black body and red hourglass shape. Often hides in dark, undisturbed areas like garages, woodpiles, and crawlspaces. Bites can cause severe muscle pain and require medical care.

Brown Recluse

Venomous / High Risk

Distinguished by the violin marking on its back. Prefers dark, dry, sheltered spots like closets, attics, and unused boxes. Bites can cause necrotic skin damage and require prompt attention.

Wolf Spider

Medium Risk / Nuisance

Large, hairy spiders that hunt prey on foot rather than building webs. Often enter ground floors or basements in search of insects. Although large and intimidating, their venom is not highly toxic to humans.

Cellar Spider

Low Risk / Common

Also known as daddy longlegs. Commonly found hanging upside down in dark cellar corners, ceiling eaves, and closets. They produce extensive messy webs but are completely harmless to humans.
