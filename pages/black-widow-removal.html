The Danger of Black Widows

Black widows are the most venomous spiders commonly found in North America. Their venom is highly toxic and can cause severe muscle cramping, pain, nausea, and respiratory complications. While rarely fatal to healthy adults, the bites pose significant danger to young children, the elderly, and household pets.

In Huntington Park, these spiders thrive in dry, sheltered areas. They often remain hidden during the day in woodpiles, under decks, inside meter boxes, or tucked away in cluttered garages. If you suspect black widow activity, do not attempt to handle them yourself. Always seek professional identification and removal.

Common Hiding Spots

  • Inside dark garages and sheds
  • Underneath outdoor furniture
  • Within woodpiles and debris
  • Inside water and gas meter boxes
  • Near foundation vents and crawl spaces

Signs of Black Widow Infestation

Irregular Webs

Unlike symmetric orb webs, black widow webs are chaotic, irregular, and very strong or sticky. They are typically built near ground level in dark corners.

Spiky Egg Sacs

Finding smooth, pale yellow or tan silken egg sacs suspended in a web is a major warning sign. A single sac can contain hundreds of baby spiders.

Nighttime Activity

Black widows are nocturnal. If you see shiny, black spiders hanging upside down in their webs at night, you likely have an infestation.
